Digital Identity Regulations S.R.O. 1956 of 2025

CNIC Services

The Government of Pakistan, through the Ministry of Interior and the National Database and Registration Authority (NADRA), has issued the Digital Identity Regulations, 2025 under the powers conferred by Section 45 of the National Database and Registration Authority Ordinance, 2000.

These regulations extend to the whole of Pakistan and come into force upon their publication in the Official Gazette.

Chapter I – Preliminary
Short Title and Commencement

These regulations may be called the NADRA Digital Identity Regulations, 2025.

Definitions

For the purposes of these regulations, unless the context otherwise requires:

Authentication refers to the process of confirming an individual’s legal identity by matching credentials such as passwords, PINs, tokens, or biometric data against stored identity records.

Authentication Service means a service provided by NADRA to verify identity data, demographic information, or biometric information of a Digital ID user and return a confirmation response or electronic Know Your Customer (e-KYC) data.

Authority refers to the National Database and Registration Authority established under the NADRA Ordinance, 2000, which is responsible for issuing, renewing, regulating, and maintaining Digital Identity credentials.

Alien Requesting Entity means any foreign individual, organization, or government agency that is not registered under the laws of Pakistan and seeks access to identity verification or authentication services.

Biometric Data includes personal data resulting from technical processing of physical or behavioral characteristics such as fingerprints, facial recognition data, and iris scans.

Card refers to the Computerized National Identity Card (CNIC) and National Identity Card for Overseas Pakistanis (NICOP), excluding Pakistan Origin Card and Alien Registration Card.

Consent means a freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication by an individual, or their guardian, agreeing to the processing of personal data for a defined purpose. Broad or vague terms of use do not constitute valid consent.

Citizen means a Pakistani national who has attained the age of eighteen years, including both resident and non-resident citizens.

Data refers to any personal, non-personal, or aggregated information collected, processed, stored, or transmitted by NADRA, whether in electronic or physical form.

Data Breach means unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, destruction, or loss of data.

Digital Identity (Digital ID) refers to legally verifiable digital credentials issued by NADRA to eligible individuals with verified biometrics, enabling access to digital services and transactions.

Digital Vault is a secure and encrypted repository under the control of the citizen for storing and sharing digital credentials and records.

Electronic Know Your Customer (e-KYC) is an electronic verification service provided by NADRA using biometric data or one-time passwords to authenticate identity and issue verifiable credentials.

National Data Exchange Layer (NDEL) is a secure digital framework that facilitates standardized data sharing among government entities while preserving data ownership and privacy.

National Data Warehouse (NDW) refers to databases maintained by NADRA under Section 7 of the Ordinance.

Requesting Entity means an agency that submits identity data for authentication or verification.

Single Sign-On (SSO) allows a user to access multiple related systems after a single authentication, as defined under international standards.

User refers to a citizen who possesses and uses a Digital ID for identity verification and access to services.

Chapter II – Regulation of Digital Identity
Issuance and Regulation

NADRA shall regulate, maintain, and issue Digital IDs to eligible citizens possessing valid identity cards and verifiable biometrics upon request.

Terms and Conditions

A Digital ID may be suspended if the user no longer meets eligibility criteria or if the underlying identity card expires or is blocked. Reactivation is subject to renewal or unblocking of the card.

User Onboarding

Citizens must onboard through NADRA’s official mobile application by completing registration and verification steps. Identity verification may include personal details, biometric data, email address, and mobile number.

Chapter III – Accessibility and Security
Accessibility for Persons with Disabilities

NADRA shall ensure that Digital ID systems are accessible to persons with disabilities. Public and private entities integrating Digital ID services must provide reasonable accommodations and assistive support.

Multilingual Accessibility

Digital ID services shall be available in Urdu, English, and other regional languages as specified by the Constitution. Users shall be provided with a clear option to select their preferred language.

Chapter IV – Consent and Data Protection
Consent Notice

Before accessing personal data for authentication or verification, requesting entities must inform users about:

The type and purpose of data being collected

The legal grounds and duration of data processing

Disclosure to third parties, if applicable

Assurance that cross-border data transfer will not occur without explicit consent

Chapter III – Consent Management and Data Security
Data Sharing

User data shall not be disclosed by any third party without the explicit written consent of the Authority. Data sharing must occur through secure and encrypted channels, ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and protection against unauthorised access, tampering, or cyber threats. Requesting entities must strictly comply with prohibitions related to biometric data as outlined in the Data Sharing Regulations.

Data Security

Requesting entities must ensure that all data centres, cloud storage facilities, servers, and information systems used for handling user data or authentication services are located within Pakistan.

All NADRA-issued identifiers, including card numbers, must be stored exclusively within a designated encrypted vault. Such identifiers must not be replicated or stored in any other database. The vault must use strong encryption standards, role-based access controls, and maintain comprehensive audit logs in compliance with applicable laws.

All onboarded requesting entities must comply with NADRA’s data security regulations, policies, standards, specifications, and guidelines issued from time to time.

Chapter IV – Single Sign-On Mechanism
Scope and Eligibility

NADRA shall provide Single Sign-On (SSO) authentication services to onboarded requesting entities to confirm that a user is a verified citizen.

To be eligible for SSO:

The user must complete onboarding through NADRA’s official mobile application.

The user must possess a valid Digital ID issued after successful verification.

User Consent for SSO

When a user attempts to access an online service using SSO, they shall be prompted to provide explicit and informed consent authorising the sharing of limited profile data with the requesting entity. Consent notices must be provided in English, Urdu, or any other language specified under the Constitution, with a clear option for language selection.

Security Compliance

Strong authentication mechanisms, including multi-factor authentication, encryption, and secure transmission channels, must be implemented to protect user data. Any tampering or data breach involving the SSO mechanism shall be subject to penalties under applicable regulations.

Limited Disclosure of Verified Profile Data

Upon successful SSO authentication, only the following verified attributes may be shared with requesting entities:

First name

Last name

Verified card number

Mobile number verified via OTP

Email address verified via OTP

Suspension of SSO

NADRA may suspend SSO access in cases of fraud, security breaches, or regulatory violations. Users shall be notified promptly of any suspension.

Chapter V – Online and Offline Verification Services
Types of Authentication Services

NADRA shall provide the following authentication services:

Online authentication using verifiable credentials through secure public key infrastructure.

Yes or No authentication responses based on identity matching.

Electronic Know Your Customer (e-KYC) authentication using multi-factor or biometric verification.

Offline Verification

Offline verification services shall include QR code-based verification. Requesting entities must follow specifications issued by NADRA. Users may download QR codes or digital IDs through NADRA’s official mobile application.

Notification and Acknowledgement

Requesting entities must notify users of the success or failure of each authentication request. Notifications may be provided via email, SMS, secure digital channels, or written acknowledgement. Notifications must include:

Name of the requesting entity

Date and time of authentication

Authentication response

Purpose of authentication

In case of authentication failure, users must be informed clearly of the reason, such as suspended Digital ID, biometric mismatch, or locked credentials.

Storage and Retention of Authentication Data

NADRA shall store authentication transaction data, including card numbers and authentication request details. Minimum transaction logs shall be retained indefinitely in accordance with applicable electronic transaction laws.

Chapter VI – Onboarding and Responsibilities of Requesting Entities
Onboarding

Requesting entities must obtain onboarding approval from NADRA before performing authentication or verification functions. Onboarding terms include duration, renewal, fees, suspension, and procedural requirements as notified by the Authority.

Roles and Responsibilities

Onboarded requesting entities must:

Obtain valid digital certificates from accredited certification authorities.

Maintain secure infrastructure and approved technologies.

Establish secure connectivity through the National Data Exchange Layer.

Conduct regular audits and comply with NADRA’s technical standards.

Cooperate fully in investigations related to fraud or disputes.

Immediately report any suspected misuse, breach, or compromise of systems.

Not store Digital ID credentials under any circumstances.

Chapter VII – Digital Vault Management

A secure, access-controlled Digital Vault shall be provided for users to store and manage Digital ID-related data and verifiable credentials. Users shall retain exclusive control over their data, including selective disclosure.

The Digital Vault must comply with data protection and cybersecurity standards and incorporate strong encryption and authentication mechanisms.

Chapter VIII – Security Obligations and Cyber Incident Response

All personnel involved in Digital ID operations must comply with information security policies and protocols. Security breaches or unauthorised disclosures shall be subject to enforcement action.

In the event of cyber incidents, NADRA shall initiate incident reporting, response, investigation, and remediation procedures. Cyber incidents must also be reported to relevant national CERT authorities.

Onboarded requesting entities must report cybersecurity incidents within one business day and provide detailed incident information, remediation actions, and law enforcement involvement if applicable.

Chapter IX – Miscellaneous
Confidentiality

All confidential Digital ID-related documents, systems, and processes must be protected. Disclosure is limited to authorised parties only. Unauthorised sharing of confidential information shall result in enforcement action.

Authority Powers

NADRA may issue clarifications, guidelines, policies, standards, directives, and procedures to ensure effective implementation of these regulations.

Delegation

The Authority may delegate powers to designated officers or staff while retaining oversight.

Review and Severability

These regulations shall be reviewed periodically. If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue to remain in force.

Fingerprint Guidelines

Pak-ID Application System

When applying fingerprints on paper, do not roll the fingers. Place fingerprint impressions lightly using an inkpad in the designated areas.

Ensure that fingerprints are clear and not smudged. If fingerprints are smudged, reprint the verifier form and apply fingerprints again.

Fingerprints must be placed in the correct sequence as indicated on the form. The form must be filled completely, and all information provided must be accurate. Providing false information or fake documents may result in legal proceedings against the applicant.

After completing the form, scan it properly. The form must be scanned at a resolution of 600 DPI using grayscale mode. The resolution should not be increased artificially through software. Save the scanned file in JPG or JPEG format.

The maximum allowed file size for upload is 10 MB. Ensure the file size does not exceed this limit before uploading.

Fingerprints can also be captured through the Pak-ID mobile application.

Examples of unacceptable fingerprint captures include tilted impressions, fingerprints outside the designated box, inverted fingers, excessive ink, and blurred impressions. Amputated fingers should be reported accordingly.

Media Brief

Family Registration Certificate (FRC) and National Identity System Reforms

The National Database and Registration Authority (NADRA), with approval from the Federal Cabinet, has introduced amendments to the National Identity Card Rules, 2002. These reforms aim to strengthen the national identity system by making it more secure, transparent, integrated, and resistant to fraud.

These amendments apply nationwide to NADRA’s identity and family record systems. Since NADRA’s establishment in 2000, no major revisions had been made to the 2002 rules, making reforms necessary to meet current and future requirements.

Reforms in Child Registration (B-Form)

Under the revised rules, issuance of a child registration certificate (B-Form) without registration at the Union Council will no longer be permitted.

Children up to three years of age are exempt from biometric data and photographs. For children aged three to ten years, a photograph is mandatory and iris scanning may be conducted where available. For children aged ten to eighteen years, both biometric data and photographs are mandatory, along with iris scanning where facilities exist.

Each child will now be issued a separate B-Form with a mandatory expiry date. Fees have not been increased, and previously issued B-Forms remain valid. However, children applying for passports must obtain the new B-Form.

These measures will improve child identification, increase transparency in registration, enhance birth registration at Union Councils, and prevent identity fraud and child trafficking.

Reforms in Family Registration Certificate (FRC)

The Family Registration Certificate has now been granted legal status. Applicants must submit a sworn declaration confirming the accuracy of family information provided.

FRCs will be issued strictly based on NADRA’s existing records. Individuals cannot obtain an FRC by providing unverified or self-declared information. Family members such as parents, spouse, siblings, and children will be included in a single certificate.

Citizens are required to ensure that unregistered family members are added to the NADRA database. Corrections to family records can be made through NADRA offices or online services.

False information may result in legal action. These reforms will ensure accuracy of records and prevent misuse of family data.

Identification Facilities for Married Women

Married women are now allowed to choose whether their father’s name or husband’s name appears on their identity card.

Seizure, Verification, Restoration, and Cancellation of Identity Documents

Revised rules introduce a structured and lawful process for verification, seizure, suspension, and cancellation of identity documents to combat fraud.

Verification boards at district, regional, and headquarters levels are required to decide cases within 30 days. Identity cards, B-Forms, and certificates may be seized or cancelled where necessary. These reforms ensure timely decisions and reduce misuse of identity documents.

Non-Chip National Identity Card Improvements

In addition to smart cards, NADRA issues a non-chip identity card with enhanced features. The card now includes bilingual information in Urdu and English, fingerprints, and a QR code instead of embedded biometrics.

This card is cheaper than the smart card and is issued faster. Processing timelines are approximately 15 days for normal, 12 days for urgent, and 6 days for executive categories. This option improves accessibility for citizens unable to afford smart cards.

Voluntary Disclosure of Incorrect Identity Information

Citizens who have knowingly or unknowingly obtained identity documents using incorrect information are encouraged to report voluntarily to NADRA. Voluntary disclosure provides legal protection, while failure to report may result in legal action if fraud is discovered.

Definitions Introduced in the Rules

Biometrics refers to personal data derived from physical or behavioral characteristics such as facial images, fingerprints, and iris scans used for unique identification.

Impounding refers to temporary suspension of an identity card or related data for investigation.

Confiscation refers to permanent seizure of an identity card or data to prevent further use.

Cancellation refers to permanent invalidation of identity documents.

Family Registration Certificate (FRC) is a legal document issued by NADRA confirming the registered family structure of an individual.

Family structures include:

Alpha family: parents and siblings

Beta family: spouse and children

Gamma family: guardian and wards

An intruder refers to a person incorrectly or unlawfully included in a family record.

Amendments to NADRA (NIC) Rules, 2002 – January 2025

Under the amended rules, disabled citizens may be issued identity cards with disability logos and lifetime validity. Minor citizens with disabilities will receive certificates or cards with prescribed validity periods.

Citizens registered as organ donors may be issued identity cards displaying donor logos. Disabled organ donors may receive combined cards reflecting both statuses.

New card formats have been introduced through additional schedules for resident and non-resident Pakistanis.

Family Registration Certificate (FRC)

NADRA may issue a Family Registration Certificate to a registered person in the prescribed format. The FRC serves as legal proof of family composition data held by NADRA at the time of issuance.

The FRC does not expire but remains valid only as long as family composition data remains unchanged. NADRA may reissue an updated certificate upon request if changes occur.

Applications for FRC must be submitted with biometric verification. NADRA may verify applicant and family member details before issuance and notify applicants of any discrepancies for correction.

Verification Boards and Their Powers

NADRA shall establish verification boards at zonal, regional, and headquarters levels. These boards have authority to verify, clear, impound, cancel, or confiscate identity cards and certificates.

Verification boards may request supporting evidence, including:

Previously issued identity cards

Certified land or domicile records

Government employment certificates

Educational records

Passports or citizenship certificates

Boards must complete hearings and decisions within thirty days of proceedings.

Digitally Marked and Impounded Data

NADRA may digitally mark a person’s data after recording reasons in writing and providing an opportunity to explain. If explanations are unsatisfactory, data may remain digitally marked until corrective action is taken or a decision is reached by the verification board.

Proceedings Under Section 18 of the Ordinance

Verification boards may initiate proceedings for return, cancellation, impounding, or confiscation of identity documents. Individuals must be served written notices and given an opportunity to respond and appear for hearings.

Decisions must be reasoned and issued within thirty days of the hearing. Appeals may be filed with the Federal Government within the prescribed time.

Removal of Intruder from Family Record

A family head may request removal of an intruder from family records by submitting an affidavit. NADRA may segregate the intruder’s data and initiate verification proceedings.

Cases involving biometric verification by blood relatives or matters pending before courts shall not be processed under this provision.

Declaration of Ineligibility

If a person voluntarily declares ineligibility for holding an identity card or certificate, NADRA may confiscate and cancel the document and impound the associated data immediately.

NADRA (National Data Repository) Regulations, 2024
Establishment of National Data Repository (NDR)

The National Data Repository functions as a centralized data repository built upon the National Data Warehouse. It enables data integration and sharing among government entities and authorized agencies for lawful purposes including governance, planning, development, identification, and security.

NDR Team

An NDR Team is constituted to manage and oversee the repository. It includes:

Head of the NDR Team

Members from NADRA

Representatives of user or feeder agencies

Secretary to the Team

The Team reports to the NADRA Authority and submits periodic performance reports.

Functions of the NDR Team

The NDR Team is responsible for:

Developing data sharing policies and SOPs

Ensuring data security and confidentiality

Managing data import and sharing

Coordinating with user and feeder agencies

Monitoring compliance and security controls

Authorized Entities and Data Sharing

Entities seeking access to data must apply in writing and demonstrate necessity and legal compliance.

Upon approval:

A Data Sharing Agreement is executed

Data use is limited to approved purposes

Security, confidentiality, and retention obligations apply

Data subject consent is not required for data sharing under the Ordinance, but privacy protections must be upheld.

Data Security and Retention

Authorized entities must:

Implement technical and organizational safeguards

Retain data only for the approved duration

Dispose of data securely after expiry

Report any data breaches or security incidents

NADRA may terminate data sharing agreements for misuse or non-compliance.

National Data Exchange Layer (NDEL) Regulations, 2025
Overview

The National Data Exchange Layer (NDEL) is a secure and interoperable digital framework established by NADRA to enable standardized data sharing between government entities and authorized public or private organizations.

These regulations apply throughout Pakistan.

Key Definitions (Cleaned)

NDEL: Secure platform for standardized data exchange without central data storage.

Onboarded Entity: Authorized public or private organization allowed to access NDEL.

Digital ID: Verifiable digital identity issued by NADRA.

Consent: Explicit, informed, and affirmative permission given by a citizen.

Biometric Data: Fingerprints, facial recognition, iris scans.

Data Breach: Unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss of data.

PKI Certificate: Cryptographic certificate issued by a National Certification Authority.

Regulation of NDEL

NADRA is responsible for:

Operation and maintenance of NDEL

Issuing technical standards and protocols

Ensuring interoperability, security, and legal compliance

Only onboarded entities may access NDEL.

Entity Onboarding Process

To access NDEL, an entity must:

Submit a formal onboarding request

Meet technical and security requirements

Obtain a PKI certificate

Execute required agreements

Specify services to be consumed or provided

Access is granted through secure APIs, and all transactions are logged.

Consent Management

Citizen data cannot be shared without explicit consent

Consent is obtained through:

Digital ID

Mobile-based secure confirmation

Consent logs are:

Cryptographically recorded

Time-stamped

Auditable

Suspension or Revocation of Access

NADRA may suspend or revoke NDEL access if an entity:

Violates laws or regulations

Poses a cybersecurity risk

Engages in fraud or misuse

Fails compliance or security standards

Entities must be given a reasonable opportunity to be heard.

Data Hosting & Sovereignty

All NDEL infrastructure and data must remain within Pakistan

Cross-border hosting, mirroring, or processing is prohibited

Ensures national data sovereignty and legal compliance

Lawful Data Sharing

Data sharing through NDEL must:

Serve lawful and defined purposes

Use minimum required data

Be fully auditable

Prohibit resale or secondary usage

Citizen consent is mandatory except in legally justified cases such as:

National security

Court orders

Criminal investigations

Data Breach Notification

If a breach occurs:

Entity must notify NADRA within 12 hours

Must submit:

Nature of breach

Data affected

Consequences

Remedial actions

Entities must maintain a Data Breach Response Plan

Transaction Logs & Audits

NADRA maintains logs for API-based access

Onboarded entities must:

Keep immutable transaction logs

Retain logs for 5 years

Allow audits by NADRA or certified auditors

Audit costs are borne by the entity.

PART A: NDEL Security, Compliance, and Enforcement
Security Obligations of Personnel

All NADRA personnel involved in the management, operation, or maintenance of the National Data Exchange Layer (NDEL) must comply with information security policies, technical guidelines, standard operating procedures, and security protocols issued by the Authority.

In case of a security breach involving personnel, disciplinary action may be taken after providing the concerned individual a reasonable opportunity of hearing.

Security Obligations of Onboarded Entities

Entities onboarded to NDEL must:

Comply with all information security policies and standards issued by NADRA

Periodically report compliance status

Protect data exchanged through NDEL from unauthorized access, loss, alteration, or disclosure

Maintain confidentiality of technical and operational information

Ensure employees sign non-disclosure agreements

Conduct background checks and restrict access to authorized personnel only

The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) oversees security implementation and compliance.

Cyber Incident Response by NADRA

NADRA must implement robust security controls to protect NDEL from cyber threats.
In case of an incident, NADRA shall conduct:

Incident reporting

Incident response and recovery

Investigation and remedial action

Cyber Incident Response by Onboarded Entities

Onboarded entities must notify NADRA within 12 hours of becoming aware of:

Cybersecurity incidents

Data breaches

Fraud or system compromise

Notification must include:

Entity name and contact details

Affected NDEL services

Nature, scope, date, time, severity, and status of the incident

Remedial actions taken

Law-enforcement referrals, if any

Incomplete information may be submitted initially, with remaining details provided within 24 hours.

All cyber incidents must also be reported to the relevant Sectoral CERT.

PART B: Compliance and Penalties
Compliance Obligations of Onboarded Entities

Onboarded entities must:

Maintain approved infrastructure and security standards

Use NADRA-approved technologies only

Obtain approval before outsourcing NDEL-related operations

Cooperate fully in audits and investigations

Remain responsible for third-party compliance

Failure to cooperate constitutes an offence under the NADRA Ordinance.

Non-Compliance and Penalties

An onboarded entity may face penalties if it:

Violates technical or security standards

Uses NDEL for unauthorized purposes

Fails to provide required information

Obstructs audits or investigations

Penalties may include suspension or termination after providing an opportunity of hearing.
Upon termination, all NDEL services and Digital ID branding must cease immediately.

Offences and Enforcement

Unauthorized access, data misuse, cyber attacks, or fraud involving NDEL systems constitute offences under the Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act, 2016.

NADRA Procurement Regulations 2025

Procurement governance, bidding procedures, contract management, compliance, and grievance redressal under NADRA

CLEAN CONTENT (RAG-READY)
1. Scope and Applicability

These regulations apply to all procurement of goods, works, services, and consultancy conducted by NADRA headquarters, regional offices, projects, and NADRA-owned entities. They also apply to procurements financed through NADRA budgets, projects, or donor funds, unless donor agreements mandate alternative procedures approved by the Authority Board. Procurements completed before enforcement remain valid if compliant with prior rules.

2. Core Definitions (Simplified)

Authority: National Database and Registration Authority established under NADRA Ordinance, 2000

Bidder: Any person or entity submitting a bid

Blacklisted: Entity barred due to fraud, corruption, or serious performance failure

Competitive Bidding: Open national or international bidding process

Data-Sensitive Procurement: Procurement involving biometric, personal, classified, or national data

Framework Agreement: Long-term procurement arrangement with defined terms

Most Advantageous Bid: Best evaluated bid based on cost, quality, and compliance

Procurement Committee: Oversight body for procurement decisions

Undefined terms follow PPRA Rules and NADRA Ordinance.

3. Procurement Governance Structure

NADRA’s Procurement Department acts as the central authority for planning, tendering, evaluation coordination, vendor management, and reporting. Standing committees include:

Procurement Committee

Grievance Redressal Committee

Blacklisting Committee

All committees operate under approved terms of reference.

4. Planning and Transparency

Each NADRA unit must prepare an Annual Procurement Plan (APP) within 30 days of budget approval. The APP includes estimated cost, procurement method, funding source, and timelines. Approved plans are published on NADRA and PPRA websites, subject to classification exceptions.

Unplanned procurements require a Supplementary Procurement Plan with justification.

Artificial splitting of procurements to bypass thresholds is prohibited.

5. Advertisement and Bidding

Open competitive bidding is the default method. Advertisements must:

Be published on e-PADS and PPRA

Appear in national newspapers for procurements above PKR 3 million

Allow minimum response time (13 days national, 30 days international)

Pre-bid meetings may be conducted for complex procurements. All clarifications and addenda must be published formally.

6. Bid Opening and Evaluation

Bids are opened publicly by a Bid Opening Committee. Evaluation follows strictly the criteria published in bidding documents. Evaluation stages include:

Preliminary examination

Technical evaluation

Financial evaluation

Post-qualification verification

No unpublished criteria may be applied. The Bid Evaluation Report (BER) is published at least 15 days before contract award.

7. Award and Contract Execution

Contracts are awarded through a Letter of Acceptance (LOA). Successful bidders must submit performance security, usually up to 10% of contract value. Contracts must be in writing and vetted where necessary.

Failure to submit required securities may result in forfeiture and award to the next eligible bidder.

8. Contract Management

Each contract is assigned a Contract Manager (CM) responsible for execution, monitoring, inspection, and payments. Key controls include:

Inspection and Acceptance Reports

Performance guarantees

Liquidated damages for delays

Variation orders capped at 15% without Chairman approval

All payments are made through banking channels only.

9. Procurement Methods

Permissible methods include:

Open Competitive Bidding

Direct Contracting (limited conditions only)

Request for Quotations (≤ PKR 500,000)

Petty Purchases (≤ PKR 100,000)

Negotiated Tendering (exceptional cases)

Force Account (state-owned execution)

Each method requires written justification and approval.

10. Consultancy Services

Consultancy is procured using:

Quality and Cost Based Selection (QCBS)

Quality Based Selection (QBS)

Least Cost Selection (LCS)

Fixed Budget Selection

Single Source Selection (exceptional, Chairman approval required)

Consultants must sign NDAs and comply with NADRA data protection policies.

11. Integrity, Ethics, and Conflict of Interest

Employees and consultants must disclose conflicts of interest. Participation in procurement where conflict exists is prohibited. Breaches may lead to disciplinary action, termination, or blacklisting.

12. Blacklisting and Debarment

Suppliers may be blacklisted for fraud, misrepresentation, breach of contract, or security violations. Due process includes notice, hearing, and documented decision. Blacklisting is communicated to PPRA.

13. Grievance Redressal

Bidders may file complaints within prescribed timelines through e-PADS. Complaints are reviewed by the Grievance Redressal Committee (GRC). Decisions are binding, subject to appeal before PPRA.

14. Emergency and Classified Procurements

Emergency procurement is allowed only under declared emergencies. Classified procurements involving national security follow restricted procedures with security-cleared vendors and enhanced confidentiality.

15. Record Retention and Audit

All procurement and contract records must be retained for at least five years and remain available for audit, investigation, and oversight.



Insufficient Data Security and Breach Notification Protocols

There is no comprehensive framework specifically addressing data security measures for biometric data, nor are there mandatory breach notification protocols. No law explicitly governs biometric privacy.

Impacts:

Increased Vulnerability: Without stringent security controls, biometric data is more susceptible to breaches and unauthorized access.

Delayed Response: The absence of mandatory breach notification mechanisms can delay incident response, worsening the impact of data breaches.

Lack of Centralized Supervisory Authority

There is no single, independent authority dedicated to overseeing biometric data protection. Regulatory oversight is fragmented across multiple bodies.

Impacts:

Inconsistent Enforcement: Fragmentation leads to uneven application of data protection standards.

Coordination Challenges: Overlapping responsibilities cause delays, inefficiencies, and accountability gaps.

Missing Biometrics in National Records

NADRA began biometric enrollment in 2005. However:

Approximately 1.2 million photographs and 2.7 million fingerprints are missing.

Photograph acquisition for women was optional until 2011.

There are significant biometric gaps for minors and non-nationals.

Impacts:

Security Risks: Inability to verify identities increases the risk of false or duplicate identities.

Access to Services: Many services (banking, telecom, government assistance) rely on biometrics. Individuals without biometric records face exclusion.

Human Trafficking Risks: Minors and women without biometric records are more vulnerable to exploitation.

Fake IDs Issued Before Biometric Implementation

Prior to biometric systems, NADRA’s registration process suffered from major weaknesses.

Issues Identified:

Identity theft and fraud were common.

Manual data entry caused inaccuracies in names, addresses, and dates of birth.

The system was vulnerable to tampering and corruption.

Lack of transparency enabled misuse and abuse of authority.

e-KYC Based on a Single Biometric Modality

Single-modality biometric e-KYC systems suffer from multiple weaknesses.

Key Limitations:

Higher risk of spoofing and security breaches

False non-match errors

Poor inclusivity for elderly and persons with disabilities

Lack of liveness detection

Bias and scalability limitations

Impacts:

Increased fraud risk

User frustration

Service exclusion for vulnerable populations

Challenges in Capturing Physiological Biometrics
Fingerprints

Weak or missing fingerprints due to age, labor, disability, or medical conditions

Environmental factors (dust, humidity, temperature) affect quality

Need for liveness-enabled scanners

Face

Temporary or permanent facial injuries

Cultural and religious objections to photography

Iris

Environmental sensitivity (lighting, dust, humidity)

Genetic eye diseases affecting enrollment

Eye surgeries and cosmetic lenses impacting recognition accuracy

Identity Theft Loopholes (Classical Examples)

Multiple registrations of the same child under different identities

Passport issuance to non-nationals under 18 without biometrics

Family tree manipulation for financial or migration benefits

Declaring relatives deceased to remove duplicate records

Fake marriages for human trafficking

SIM and bank account issuance without proper biometric verification

Misuse of Biometrics (2014 Case Study)

During early biometric SIM verification:

Silicone and fake fingerprints were used to bypass systems

Fingerprints were collected through donation drives and copied onto films

Even multi-fingerprint systems were defeated using paper prints and oil

Biometric data leaks from other systems (e.g., driving license databases) were exploited

Outcome:
Multi-modal biometric verification was introduced to mitigate these risks.

Core Problem Statement

Responsibility for vital event reporting lies with citizens, not the state

Multiple unintegrated systems exist across NADRA, Union Councils, and other agencies

Repeated manual data entry causes duplication and inconsistency

Around 40 million people remain unregistered

NADRA service centers are sparse, causing access and capacity issues

No standardized national biometric or registration policy exists

Opportunities

Standardization can create a single, authoritative source of truth

Integration of NADRA and Union Council systems will reduce illegal registrations

Continuous population registration can eliminate the need for traditional census

Extending services to Union Councils will drastically improve accessibility

Mobile-based services can leverage high mobile and broadband penetration

NADRA Ordinance 2000 – Fund, Accounts, Audit and Penalties


Financial Management, Audit, Confidentiality, Offences, Governance

CLEAN CONTENT
NADRA Fund

A non-lapsable fund called the NADRA Fund is established to finance the Authority’s functions and operations. This includes salaries and remuneration of the Chairman, members, registration officers, staff, consultants, advisers, and other employees.

The Fund consists of grants, loans, foreign aid, service fees, income from property, securities issued by the Authority, and all other lawful receipts. The Fund is maintained in one or more bank accounts in Pakistan or abroad and operated according to NADRA regulations.

The Federal Government exclusively funds NADRA’s operations for at least three years from establishment. After this period, funding responsibility may cease if NADRA becomes self-sufficient, subject to formal notification.

Budget and Accounts

NADRA must submit an annual budget showing estimated receipts and expenditures for Federal Government approval. Any surplus after tax is remitted to the Federal Consolidated Fund, while deficits are covered by the Federal Government.

NADRA maintains accurate accounts and undergoes audits conducted by chartered accountants appointed by the Federal Government in consultation with the Auditor General of Pakistan.

Confidentiality of Information

Any person who unlawfully discloses information obtained through employment with NADRA is punishable by imprisonment up to five years, a fine up to one million rupees, or both. Disclosure is permitted only for criminal proceedings or when authorized by NADRA.

Unauthorized use, access, or interference with NADRA’s registration systems, databases, or data infrastructure is punishable by rigorous imprisonment up to fourteen years and a minimum fine of one million rupees.

Offences and Penalties

Offences include failure to register, concealment of information, failure to report lost cards, refusal to surrender identity documents, late application for CNIC, or violation of NADRA rules. Penalties include imprisonment up to six months or fines.

Serious offences such as false statements, tampering with identity cards, forging documents, possessing multiple cards unlawfully, or fraudulent attestation are punishable by imprisonment up to five years and fines up to one hundred thousand rupees.

All fines collected are credited to the Federal Consolidated Fund.

Legal Proceedings

Courts may only take cognizance of offences upon a written complaint by NADRA or an authorized officer. Trials are conducted by Magistrates of the First Class, including summary trials where applicable.

Governance and Employment

The Chairman and members are paid emoluments determined by the Federal Government and may not engage in outside employment, business, or advisory roles related to NADRA projects. They must not hold financial interests in entities dealing with NADRA.

NADRA may appoint registration officers, staff, consultants, and advisers as required. All such personnel are deemed public servants under Pakistani law.

Transfer of Employees and Assets

Upon NADRA’s establishment, employees of predecessor organizations were transferred to NADRA with preserved service conditions. Assets, liabilities, contracts, and legal proceedings were also transferred to NADRA.

Employees could opt out of transfer within specified periods. No compensation is payable solely due to transfer.

Tax Exemption

NADRA is exempt from income tax, super tax, and wealth tax for five years from establishment.

Rule-Making Authority

The Federal Government may issue rules, and NADRA may issue regulations, to implement the Ordinance. NADRA regulations override other laws where conflicts exist.

Removal of Difficulties and Repeal

The Federal Government may issue orders to resolve implementation difficulties. The National Registration Act, 1973, is repealed, but prior actions remain valid unless inconsistent with the Ordinance.


NADRA Identity Card Renewal, CRC Services, and Legal Undertakings


Identity Card Renewal, CRC Application, Biometric Verification, Legal Undertakings

CLEAN CONTENT
Identity Card Renewal (PakID Mobile App)

Citizens can renew their identity card using the NADRA PakID mobile application. The process begins by selecting the ID Card option under Identity Documents and choosing Renew.

The applicant must first identify whether the application is for:

Self, or

A blood relative (parents, siblings, spouse, or minors)

If applying for a relative, the applicant must enter the relative’s citizen number.

The system then guides the applicant through biometric verification. Facial verification is attempted first. If facial verification is successful, fingerprint verification is optional. If required, fingerprints are captured using the phone camera under guided instructions.

Applicants select a processing category:

Normal

Urgent

Executive

The delivery method for the renewed card must be selected. Options include delivery to a permanent address, temporary address, another specified address, or collection from a selected NADRA center.

After uploading any required supporting documents, the application is submitted. Payment is requested only after background verification and approval. The applicant is notified through the app once payment is required.

Child Registration Certificate (CRC) Application

A New CRC can be applied for using the PakID mobile app by selecting the CRC option.

The applicant must identify whether the application is for self or a blood relative. Processing priority (Normal or Executive) is selected before proceeding.

For newborns or minors, the applicant must provide:

Child’s full name (English and Urdu)

Gender and date of birth

Birth country, province, district, tehsil

Relationship with applicant

Twin status (if applicable)

Parents’ details are mandatory and include citizen numbers and names in English and Urdu.

Disability details, if applicable, may be added by entering the disability type, certificate number, and issuance date. This section is optional.

Biometric attestation by the spouse or parent is required to complete the CRC application. Required documents must be uploaded clearly and legibly. The application is reviewed and submitted after confirmation.

Biometric Capture Guidelines

Biometric verification may include facial recognition and fingerprint capture.

For fingerprint capture:

Hands must be steady and properly aligned under the camera

Fingers should not be spread, tilted, or curled

Background must be plain with no shadows

The phone should not be shaken during capture

Lower-specification devices may take additional time during biometric verification.

Legal Undertakings (Standardized Summaries)
CNIC or NICOP Cancellation

Applicants holding foreign nationality may voluntarily cancel CNIC or NICOP when applying for a Pakistan Origin Card (POC). The applicant declares acquisition of foreign nationality and relinquishes future claims to Pakistani citizenship.

POC Cancellation

Dual nationals who mistakenly obtained a POC may request cancellation by declaring continued Pakistani citizenship and intent to retain CNIC or NICOP.

Lost Card Declaration

Applicants declare loss of CNIC, NICOP, or Smart ID and undertake to return the card if found later.

Marital Status Declarations

Applicants may declare marital status changes including:

Marriage

Divorce

Widow or widower status

Declarations affirm the correctness of marital information and acceptance of legal responsibility for false statements.

Name Change

Applicants may declare a name change and confirm that the name will not be reverted or modified again, except where legally allowed.

Religion Correction or Conversion

Applicants may request correction of religion in NADRA records or declare conversion to Islam. Declarations affirm correctness and legal responsibility.

NICOP Without Passport

Applicants residing abroad without a valid passport may submit an undertaking explaining the reason, such as loss or absence of passport, subject to verification by Pakistani missions.

Correction Form (General Guidance)

Correction requests must clearly specify only the field being corrected, such as name or date of birth. Applicants must affirm that information provided is accurate and acknowledge legal consequences for false declarations.
